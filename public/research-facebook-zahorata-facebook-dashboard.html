Key Findings -- Facebook Algorithm (2026)

1. Posting Volume Gap

Za Horata posted 3 times in 14 days. Every major competitor posts daily or more (10-20 posts/14 days). On Facebook, the algorithm's first-hour "testing window" means each post is an opportunity to enter the recommendation engine. With only 3 posts, Za Horata had only 3 chances in 2 weeks.

Benchmark: The optimal Facebook Page posting frequency is 3-5 posts per week (about 6-10 in 14 days). Za Horata is at 30% of minimum.

2. Engagement Quality Analysis

Za Horata's 3.2% engagement rate is actually decent -- above ГЕРБ and ДБ. But with only 950 views, the absolute numbers are too small to trigger algorithmic amplification. Facebook requires strong engagement in the first 6 hours to boost a post. With few followers, this is the cold-start problem.

Top performer by engagement rate: Министерски съвет (47.4%) -- driven by high comment volume (26,116 comments on 20 posts, averaging 1,306 comments each). This shows the algorithm rewards comment-heavy content above all.

3. Content Format Analysis

Za Horata is 100% video (Reels). This is partially correct -- Reels get the highest reach on Facebook. But the top performers use a mixed strategy:

  • Радев: 30% video, 70% photos/text -- 2M views. Photos get 35% more Feed engagement than text and 44% more than in-feed video.
  • Борисов: 70% video -- 620K views. Heavy video works when you have an established audience.
  • Костадинов: 70% video -- 540K views, but 28.7% engagement. His confrontational style drives shares (13,818) which are the #1 algorithm signal.
  • Мая Манолова: 17% video -- 146K views from only 12 posts. Proves photo/text posts work well for political pages.

Key insight: Facebook's algorithm in 2026 rewards format diversity. Mix Reels + photos + text posts + carousels. The algorithm scans your last 9-12 posts to define your "brand tag" -- all-video makes you a video account, limiting feed placement.

Facebook's #1 algorithm signal in 2026: Private sharing via Messenger/WhatsApp. This is invisible in public metrics but drives massive reach. Shares are the visible proxy -- Za Horata has essentially zero.

5. Political Content on Facebook -- Special Rules

  • Meta estimates political content is less than 3% of total Feed views (down from 6% in 2020). Political pages face a structural disadvantage.
  • Political/civic content is classified as a "sensitive topic" in Facebook's Recommendation Guidelines -- it gets reduced distribution to non-followers.
  • However, in March 2026 Meta reversed course: referral traffic to publishers quadrupled vs March 2025. Political content is getting more reach again.
  • Paid promotion of political content requires "Paid for by" disclaimers and authorization via Meta's Ad Library. AI-generated political ads must disclose AI use.

6. Facebook Groups Opportunity

Facebook Groups have 1.8 billion monthly users and deliver significantly higher organic reach than Pages. Group posts rank higher in Feed. Za Horata has no Group presence -- this is the single biggest missed opportunity on Facebook.

Facebook Promotion Strategy (Different from TikTok!)

Facebook paid promotion for political content has strict rules:

  • Step 1: Apply for "Social Issues, Elections or Politics" ad authorization (requires ID verification, Bulgarian address, "Paid for by" disclaimer)
  • Step 2: All political ads appear in Meta Ad Library for 7 years -- full transparency
  • Step 3: AI-generated content in political ads must be disclosed since 2024
  • Step 4: Budget: Start with 5-10 EUR/day on best-performing organic posts. Facebook minimum viable boost is 1 EUR/day but 5 EUR gives meaningful reach
  • Budget allocation: 80% organic (posting 5-7x/week, engaging in comments, Group activity) + 20% paid (30-50 EUR/month boosting 2-3 best posts)
